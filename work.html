The Collection

Fox nap

A restful evening has invited this little fox to nap in a shady corner of the woods. Captured in acrylic on cardboard.

Lunar Moth

Who has ever seen these beautiful creatures find their way to a window or lampost and not been taken by their gentle color and light fluttering? They seem to reflect light as if they were tiny moons, which gives them a heavenly glow. Mixed media on cardboard.

Woodland Cottage

A delightfully quaint little cottage nestled amongst the trees and bushes. It speaks of serenity and cool spring days. Painted in watercolor on paper.

Brooklyn's Giraffe

My neice loves giraffe. She asked me some time ago to paint one for her, and of course I could not refuse. I had never painted a giraffe, and really had never been interested. As I studied them I found them delightfully facinating, from the way their strangely long neck balances, to the fuzzy horns they bear, and of course that delightful pattern! What an interesting discovery they were. The experience further reinforced why we should be open to new ideas and experiences, even if they do not at first seem to align with our usual interests. Painted in watercolor.

Yellow House

In addition to nature painting, I've always been interested in buildings. This one carries a touch of whimsy and playfulness, with bright colors and exaggerated proportions. Painted in watercolor with graphite on paper.

Mossy Path

Along one my favorite trails there are countless pleasant curves and hills to explore. They can seem almost endless, allowing one to wander til their heart’s content. This lovely day the green of the grass and moss seemed to shift the light into a most peaceful shade. Captured here in mixed media on paper.

Path Home

Each day I return home I am greeted by this quaint graveled, grassy path. It is a little bumpy and uneven. Weeds and bushes are constantly fighting for a place along it’s edges. Trimming and keeping such a long path can be quite a chore, but it brings me joy each and every time I return. It is the first to greet me and welcome me home, pleasantly sheltered by the shade of trees along either side. Drawn in Graphite in my sketchbook.

Screech Owl

If one has ever heard the call of a screech owl, it might be surprising to know how small they actually are. We have a surprising number of owls around us, but our little neighbor is certainly the most talkative of the crew. The little owl seems to welcome each evening as the time to search for the nightly dinner is announced, warning away any others that might be planning on encroaching on the territory which has already been laid claim by our little guardian. I think this work took a subconciously impressionist turn, as the thought of the sounds and calls of our little neighbor certainly influenced the wild, sketchy lines and jagged marks that found their way onto the page. Work in ink and Graphite on tinted paper.

Cat Nap

This is one of my illustration-style pieces: an attempt to capture a lazy, warm day in which our little feline friend has found a delightfully shady spot in a quiet corner of town in which to nap. If I recall now, this was inspired by animation cell image, though that piece has long since been lost to me. I love the way the light plays across the fruit, the stones, and the edges of the leaves that it reaches. Ink and watercolor on paper.

Sunflower

We had a patch of sunflowers grow and bloom from a corner of our house one year where the birds had been particularly picky, messy eaters, tossing many of the sunflower seeds onto the ground. I did enjoy watching them track the sun, and one particularly sunny day was inspired to catch one as it stared up into the vibrant blue sky. This piece has an impressionist air to it, most notably the ’ripples’ in the sky. I do enjoy that dreamy haze of the chalk pastels. Chalk pastel on paper.

Anticipation

Our sweet cat Niki, as many cats do, has such a peculiar way that she stares at me while we discuss her needs. I am quite certain that I have never fully understood what it is she is after, but as she stares at me with anticipation each time, expecting that perhaps this time she may finally have conveyed her royal orders to her rather dim subject. Alas, I think I may never get it quite right, but she playfully tolerates me and my attempts to please her all the same. A barely passable attempt to capture her lofty stare in graphite and charcoal on paper.
